Preparing For Your Driving Tests in Tapping

Before you can drive on the road, you will need a license first. To get a driver’s license, you will need to pass a driving test provided by licensing centres at the Department of Transport’s Metropolitan Driver and Vehicle Service. The test varies in difficulty depending on your skill level. Before taking any exam, it’s best to prepare for them. Repeated attempts are not only time-consuming but expensive as well.

You will need to pass a theory test regarding the traffic rules for beginners. Passing this test earns you a learner’s permit, letting your drive under the supervision of a fully licensed driver. To prepare for the exam, you will need to review the Drivers’ Handbook supplied by the Department of Transport. You’ll want to read it carefully and study everything. You can also take practice tests to help you grasp what the test is like, increasing your chances of success.

Next is the Hazard Perception Test (HPT), a computerised exam. The examiner will present you with a few clips of real-world driving scenarios, and you will need to watch out for developing hazards and act only when necessary. Unnecessary actions and failure to recognise hazards will lose you points. It’s best to get hands-on experience for this test. Apex Driving School offers driving lessons to help you develop your hazard recognition skills and improve your decision-making needed to pass your HPT.

The final and the most challenging exam is the Practical Driving Assessment (PDA). An instructor will measure your overall ability and check whether you have the skills to be a fully licensed driver. At Apex Driving School, we only recommend taking the exam when your driving instructors think you are ready. We have helped thousands of students ace the test with our effective teaching methods and helpful advice. We also suggest taking one final driving lesson with our driving instructors in Tapping.
